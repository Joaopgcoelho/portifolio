Context

The problem and the landscape

The Student Virtual Classroom (SAVA) is the primary digital touchpoint for YDUQS students. The previous home screen didn't reflect students' real needs: scattered information, confusing navigation, and no visibility into academic progress.

The product team identified an opportunity to transform the interface into a self-management tool, giving students back their time and focus.

Diagnosis

What we found

  • No visibility into course progress
  • Navigation based on internal structure, not student behavior
  • Missing shortcuts for critical actions like exams and deadlines
  • Visual inconsistency across group brands (IBMEC, Estácio)
  • "Continue where you left off" feature with insufficient accuracy
Strategy

Decisions and reasoning

We chose an approach centered on real behavioral and usage data. We reorganized the information architecture based on navigation metrics and qualitative student feedback.

"We went beyond a new screen — we delivered a strategic experience to remove day-to-day academic obstacles and centralize what truly matters."

Execution

Design and implementation

We worked in iterative cycles with continuous validation. Key deliverables included:

  • Course carousel with progress bars and an improved "Continue where you left off" feature
  • Smart Exam Panel with direct shortcuts and status filters
  • Sidebar menu reorganized based on real behavioral data
