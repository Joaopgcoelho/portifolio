Virtual Lab — Scaling hands-on education with cost reduction

How we built an immersive experience for remote nursing education, saving over $400K in operational costs.

-$400K+ Operational costs
3 months Concept to delivery
↑ Access Hands-on learning in EAD
↑ Engagement Nursing students
Product DesignMobile FirstEdTechSimulationHealthcare

Hands-on simulation — Central Sterile Supply Department (CSSD)

Context

$400K+/year on a vendor that wasn't delivering

  • YDUQS paid over $400K/year for third-party practical simulations
  • Inflexible solution — no control over content or experience
  • Access limited to specific schedules and devices
  • Pandemic accelerated the need for 100% remote hands-on learning
Insight

"The problem wasn't teaching better —
it was enabling practice without depending on physical space."

Decisions

4 bets that defined the product

📱 Mobile first

70%+ of students access via mobile

⚡ Reduced scope

CSSD only — validate before scaling

🧠 Functional simulation

Clear feedback > 3D graphics

🎯 DS integration

Less UI work, more learning experience

Execution

3 months, 3 phases

Month 1 — Discovery

CSSD mapping with specialists. Interaction architecture. Wireframes.

Month 2 — Build

High-fidelity design. Mobile-first development. DS integration.

Month 3 — Validation

Tests with real students. +40 UX adjustments. Production deploy.

My role Product Designer — discovery to delivery (UX, UI, product strategy)
Impact
-$400K+ saved per year in just 3 months of work
3 months from zero to production
100% remote access to hands-on learning
Transformation

Before and after

Before
  • $400K+/year on external vendor
  • Access limited by schedule and device
  • No control over content
  • Zero scalability
After
  • Internal solution with controlled cost
  • 24/7 access on any device
  • Own customizable content
  • Replicable model for other disciplines
Learnings

What this project taught me

  • Constraints create focus — tight deadlines eliminate unproductive discussions
  • Mobile first is inclusion — designing for mobile is designing for access
  • Specialists change everything — nurses turned UI into a real learning tool
  • MVP validates the model — CSSD success opened the path to scale
  • Financial impact opens doors — $400K+ changes the conversation about UX investment

Design isn't just about solving interfaces.

It's about turning constraints into real impact.

This project is proof of that.
